Overview



Usage-Based Insurance (UBI) is a recent innovation by auto insurers that more closely aligns driving behaviors with premium rates for auto insurance. Mileage and driving behaviors are tracked using odometer readings or in-vehicle telecommunication devices (telematics) that are usually self-installed into a special vehicle port or already integrated in original equipment installed by car manufactures. The basic idea of telematics auto insurance is that a driverâ€™s behavior is monitored directly while the person drives. iQ-Things telematics devices measure a number of elements of interest to underwriters: kilometers driven; time of day; where the vehicle is driven (GPS); rapid acceleration; hard braking; hard cornering; and airbag deployment.


iQ-Things device is equipped with 3D/6D accelerometer/Gyroscope to detect each and every event with driving behavior like rapid acceleration, hard braking, hard cornering, etc.

With an auto calibrated 3D accelerometer, iQ-Things devices are able to send immediate notifications in case of crash and rollover.



iQ-Things devices use the best possible way to offer the accurate mileage data for the insurer so that they can bill the insured person based on mileage.
